Siamese

The Siamese is a cat breed from Thailand, they are one of the oldest and most recognizable cat breeds, famous for their looks, but also for their voice [1], [2], [3].

Appearance

Siamese have a distinctive coat coloration which makes them memorable and recognizable. Their coat is “point colored“, meaning their ears, feet, and tails are darker than their body. The “point color“ is usually brown or chocolate, but in some rare cases even blueish gray or lilac. The body is a light color, usually variants of white, such as cream. Siamese is a shorthair cat breed with a particularly shiny coat. They are of medium size, weighing from 4kg to 7kg, with a very slim and long body. Along with their coat pattern, they are known for their distinctive eye color! All Siamese cats have blue eyes, its shades ranging from sky-blue to navy blue.

Personality

Siamese cats are amongst the most social and talkative cat breeds. They love nothing more than to spend time with their loved ones. As one of the chattiest cat breeds, they will tell you everything about their day, give unwanted opinions and comments, and sing at least half of the day. Siamese love attention and love to be surrounded by people, they are great companions for big families and get along with other animals and children. Therefore, it is a nightmare for them to be left home alone. Also, due to their mischievous nature, it is not recommended… Siamese thrive on companionship, it is always best to have two Siamese cats, so they can interact with each other throughout the day and not be lonely or bored since they are also very playful and active cats!

History

The Siamese originated in Siam (now Thailand), thus the breed’s name. They were considered sacred in ancient Siam, and due to that only royalty and Buddhist monks were allowed to have them. The breed was first described in the ancient Tamra Maew manuscripts which were curated during the Ayutthaya Kingdom as early as 1350. When Europeans began visiting Siam in the 1800s, members of the Siamese royal family offered the Siamese cats as gifts for the Europeans. In that way, the breed made its way to Europe, quickly gaining recognition and popularity due to its exquisite appearance. The Siamese made its debut in Europe in 1871 at the Crystal Palace Cat Show in London.
The Siamese cat breed is the parent breed for the Siamese Breed Group, which includes the Balinese, also known as the longhaired Siamese, the Oriental Shorthair, and the Oriental Longhair. They all share the same body type, but come with a different “paint job” [1], [2], [3].
